Aaron Swartz

The Child of the Internet

Aaron Swartz was not only one the founding fathers of the internet, but he also dedicated his life to fighting to protect the freedoms so many of us take for granted every day while using it.

Althought this bright young mind was taken from us at a young age, it is still important for us to remember him, and to realize how much his life has impacted us every day. He is truly of the greatest minds of the internet.

"What is the most important thing you could be working on in the world right now? And if you're not working on that, why aren't you?"

Aaron Swartz

A Brief Summary of Aaron's Life

  • 1986: Born on November 8th
  • 2000: Helped build the RSS 1.0 web syndicationm specification, at the age of only 14
  • 2005: After attending Standford University, he helped start a company known as Infogami which was a content mangement system which helped create visually pleasing websites.
  • 2005: Began working with the founders of Reddit, to help rewrite their python codebase, thus cementing him as one of the founders of Reddit. He later merged Infogami with Reddit.
  • 2006: After Reddit was brought out by Conde Nast Publications, the owners of Wired, Swartz moved to San Francisco to work at the Magazine. However, he soon found that he hated office life, and left the company.
  • 2008: Swartz founded Watchdog.net, which he described as the "good government site with teeth." He also wrote the notious Guerilla Open Access Manifest in the same year, as well as helped build SecureDrop, a platform where wistleblowers could safely communicate with journalists.
  • 2008: Swartz downloaded over 2.7 million federal court documents stored under PACER. He argued that PACER charged 8 cents per document to citizens, despite the fact that the documents were supposed to be free to the public. Although he was investigated by the FBI, no charges were ultimatly brought against him.
  • 2009: Helped launch the Progressive Change Campaign Committee, in an effort to help push more progressive agendas.
  • 2010: Co-founded Demand Progress, a political group which pushed for people to take action and contact Congress and other leaders, in order to help once again push progressive agendas.
  • 2011: Downloaded a large number of acedemic journals through JSTOR, via a computer hidden in an MIT wiring closet. He was caught on videa replacing the hard drive of the computer to allow it to download more of the journals. JSTOR pursued a lawsuit, leading to Aaron's arrest. He was indicted by a federal jury on accounts of wire fraud, computer fraud, and unlawfully obtaining information from a protected computer, and recklessly damaging a protected computer.
  • 2012: Helped in the campaign to stop the "Stop Online Piracy Act" (SOPA), which would have made it easier for the government to shut down websites, and placed a major burden on ISP's. The campaign saw several hundred sites "go dark" in solidarity with the movement.
  • 2013: On January 11th, Swartz committed suidcide, and was found in his apartment. After his death, the prosecuters dropped the charges against Swartz
